The Intelligent Cooperation and Nonlinear Control Laboratory focuses on cutting-edge research in control theory, robotics, and multi-agent systems. Our team investigates both theoretical foundations and practical implementations, aiming to advance autonomous systems, intelligent networks, and complex dynamic systems.

Cooperative Control of Multi-Agent Systems

Our research in cooperative control explores how multiple agents, such as robots or drones, can work together to achieve a common objective. We design distributed control algorithms that allow agents to communicate, share information, and coordinate their actions efficiently. Applications include cooperative output regulation control, formation control, consensus tracking, and cooperative task allocation in heterogeneous multi-agent systems. By integrating robustness and adaptability, our methods can handle dynamic environments and uncertainties in real-world scenarios.

Nonlinear Control

Nonlinear control is central to dealing with complex systems whose dynamics cannot be approximated by linear models. We study advanced nonlinear control techniques, including backstepping, sliding mode control, and adaptive control, to ensure stability, robustness, and performance. Our focus is on both theoretical developments and their applications to robotics, unmanned aerial vehicle, and networked systems, where nonlinearities and uncertainties are inherent and critical to consider.

Modeling and Control of Robots

In the area of robotics, we develop precise models and control strategies for robotic manipulators, mobile robots, and aerial systems. Our work covers kinematic and dynamic modeling, trajectory planning, and control law design. By combining simulation, experimental validation, and real-time control, we aim to improve robot accuracy, efficiency, and safety in complex tasks, including assembly and inspection.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles

Unmanned aerial vehicles present unique challenges in terms of navigation, stability, and autonomous operations. Our research focuses on control strategies for UAVs, including flight stabilization and path following. We are also developing amphibious UAVs capable of operating in both land-air and sea-air environments. Current studies also explore adaptive and nonlinear control methods to ensure stability and efficiency in uncertain or rapidly changing conditions.
